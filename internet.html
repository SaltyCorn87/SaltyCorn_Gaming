Big Picture

The Internet started as a research project designed to connect computers across long distances. Over time, packet switching, TCP/IP, and the World Wide Web turned it into a public network used for communication, business, entertainment, and online communities.

1960s–1970s: Early Networks

  • 1969: ARPANET connects early research institutions.
  • 1971: Email becomes an early “killer app.”
  • 1970s: Networking expands and more networks appear.

1980s–1990s: Standards & The Web

  • 1983: TCP/IP standard adoption supports internetworking.
  • 1984: DNS introduces domain names.
  • 1990–1993: The Web + browsers make the Internet usable for everyone.

2000s–Today: Broadband, Mobile, Cloud

  • Broadband improves speed and supports richer services.
  • Mobile Internet expands global access.
  • Cloud services power streaming, social platforms, and online gaming.

Why this matters for multiplayer gaming

Faster, more stable Internet made real-time multiplayer normal—supporting matchmaking, voice chat, live updates, and global communities.
