What is the difference between LAN and online multiplayer?

LAN (Local Area Network) multiplayer connects players on the same local network, often in the same building. Online multiplayer uses the Internet to connect players across long distances, allowing global participation.

When did online multiplayer become common?

Online multiplayer began appearing in the 1990s on PC, but it became mainstream in the 2000s when home broadband Internet became widely available and consoles introduced online services.

Why do many consoles require subscriptions for online play?

Subscription services help fund server infrastructure, account systems, matchmaking, security, and ongoing platform features. These services support stable online gaming experiences.

How did faster Internet change multiplayer gaming?

Faster speeds and lower latency made real-time multiplayer smoother and more reliable. This enabled competitive games, large online worlds, voice chat, and live updates.

What is cross-platform play?

Cross-platform play allows players on different devices, such as PC, console, and mobile, to play together in the same game environment.

What role does the cloud play in modern multiplayer?

Cloud infrastructure hosts game servers, player accounts, matchmaking systems, and updates. It allows developers to support players around the world without requiring physical hardware in every location.
