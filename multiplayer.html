What is multiplayer gaming?

Multiplayer gaming is any game experience where more than one player participates, either together in the same room (local) or connected through a network (LAN/online).

Early multiplayer

  • Local multiplayer: split-screen and shared-device play
  • Arcade competition: high scores and head-to-head cabinets

LAN multiplayer (networked locally)

LAN (Local Area Network) gaming connects nearby computers or consoles through the same local network. This was a big step because it allowed more players, faster connections, and a shared game environment without relying on the wider Internet.

Online multiplayer

Online play connects players across cities, countries, and continents. As Internet speeds improved, multiplayer shifted from small lobbies to massive online worlds, matchmaking, and persistent accounts.

  • Matchmaking: systems that automatically group players together
  • Voice/text chat: communication becomes a core feature
  • Live updates: games evolve after launch through patches and seasons

Modern era: cross-play and cloud

Today, multiplayer often supports cross-platform play (PC + console + mobile) and relies on online services for identity, matchmaking, anti-cheat, and updates. Cloud infrastructure also enables game streaming and global server coverage.
